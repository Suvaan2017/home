In Nepal, animal shelters play a crucial role in rescuing, rehabilitating, and rehoming abandoned and stray animals, primarily dogs and cats. Many animals in Nepal are left to survive on the streets due to neglect, lack of awareness, and misconceptions about breed value. Why Are So Many Animals Abandoned? One of the biggest challenges in Nepal is the abandonment of dogs and cats simply because they are not "good breeds." Many people prefer expensive, foreign breeds like Labradors, German Shepherds, or Huskies, considering them a status symbol. As a result, native Nepali breeds and mixed-breed animals are often seen as less desirable. When families realize that purebred dogs require high maintenance or when street puppies and kittens grow up and lose their "cute" appeal, they are often abandoned. Additionally, many pets are left on the streets when owners move, face financial struggles, or are unable to care for them due to lack of veterinary access.
